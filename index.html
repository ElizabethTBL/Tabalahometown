About

St. Petersburg was founded in 1703 by Russia’s first Emperor Peter I or Peter the Great. In 1712–1918, it was the capital of Russia. In 1914, the city got the name of Petrograd, while in 1924 it was renamed as Leningrad. It got its historic name back only in 1991. Nowadays, it is Russia’s second largest city after Moscow in terms of population.

Underground (Метро)

The underground in St. Petersburg is the deepest in the world, with tunnels located 70–80 metres below the ground. The deepest station is Admiralteiskaya, with a record of 86 metres. Some of SPb's underground stations are also considered ones of the most beautiful in the world.
